Interactive Co-Design of Safe and Assured Autonomous Systems

Workshop at CPS-Week 2026 | Saint Malo, France

About the Workshop

As autonomous cyber-physical systems (CPS) transition from laboratories to high-stakes environments like healthcare and defense, ensuring safety and stakeholder alignment has become a critical necessity. The emergence of large language models (LLMs) and foundation models offers a transformative opportunity to bridge the gap between diverse stakeholders and technical design. By leveraging multi-modal elicitation—including text, speech, and simulations—researchers can now translate natural human input into the formal specifications and environment models required for rigorous CPS verification and assurance.

However, effective co-design is fundamentally a human-computer interaction (HCI) challenge; tools must be carefully designed to prevent user fatigue and ensure specifications remain consistent. This workshop aims to unite researchers from the CPS, IoT, and HCI communities to develop interfaces and algorithms that allow for iterative, interpretable, and efficient requirement gathering. By integrating human factors with formal methods, the event seeks to catalyze a new research agenda for building safe, human-in-the-loop autonomous systems grounded in real-world deployment needs.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions that explore the intersection of HCI, formal methods, and autonomous systems. To encourage broad participation and the exchange of early-stage ideas, we allow dual publications or re-submission of previously published papers or case studies. We also specifically welcome negative results as long as they provide meaningful insights to spur discussion for the theme of our workshop.

Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Interactive elicitation of system requirements, task objectives, or task environments for autonomous systems
  • Safety constraints, assurance cases, and runtime safety envelopes derived from human input
  • Human–robot/system collaboration requirements in shared workspaces
  • Detecting and repairing inconsistencies, ambiguities, and missing assumptions in specifications with human-in-the-loop
  • Interpretable representations for users to inspect, debug, and correct formal artifacts
  • Verification, validation, and testing workflows supported by interactive co-design tools
  • Generating test scenarios, edge cases, and adversarial situations from stakeholder knowledge
  • Benchmark datasets, metrics, and evaluation protocols for interactive requirements tools
  • Case studies in interactive tacit knowledge elicitation in manufacturing, construction, healthcare, defense, and other high-stakes CPS deployments
  • Negative results, lessons learned, and failure modes of interactive/LLM-driven co-design
