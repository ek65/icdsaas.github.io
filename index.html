Interactive Co-Design of Safe and Assured Autonomous Systems

Workshop at CPS-Week 2026 | Saint Malo, France

About the Workshop

Cyber-physical systems (CPS), including industrial manipulators, mobile robots, autonomous vehicles, and emerging humanoid platforms, are rapidly moving from controlled laboratory settings into high-stakes application domains such as manufacturing, construction, healthcare, and defense[cite: 7, 113]. In these environments, autonomous systems increasingly share physical workspaces with human operators and must coordinate with other autonomous agents and networked infrastructure[cite: 8, 114].

This tight coupling between computation, physical dynamics, and human activity makes safety, robustness, and assurance central concerns[cite: 9, 115]. The workshop focuses on how to interactively elicit and formalize stakeholder requirements—including regulators, developers, and end users—in the design and verification of autonomous CPS[cite: 11, 12, 117, 118]. We explore how recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) and foundation models open new opportunities for interactive, multi-modal knowledge elicitation[cite: 13, 119].
