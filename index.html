Interactive Co-Design of Safe and Assured Autonomous Systems

Workshop at CPS-Week 2026 | Saint Malo, France

About the Workshop

As autonomous cyber-physical systems (CPS) transition from laboratories to high-stakes environments like healthcare and defense, ensuring safety and stakeholder alignment has become a critical necessity. The emergence of large language models (LLMs) and foundation models offers a transformative opportunity to bridge the gap between diverse stakeholders and technical design. By leveraging multi-modal elicitation—including text, speech, and simulations—researchers can now translate natural human input into the formal specifications and environment models required for rigorous CPS verification and assurance.

However, effective co-design is fundamentally a human-computer interaction (HCI) challenge; tools must be carefully designed to prevent user fatigue and ensure specifications remain consistent. This workshop aims to unite researchers from the CPS, IoT, and HCI communities to develop interfaces and algorithms that allow for iterative, interpretable, and efficient requirement gathering.

Call for Papers

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Interactive elicitation of system requirements and task objectives
  • Safety constraints and runtime safety envelopes derived from human input
  • Human–robot/system collaboration requirements in shared workspaces
  • Detecting and repairing ambiguities in specifications with human-in-the-loop
  • Interpretable representations for users to inspect and debug formal artifacts
  • Case studies in manufacturing, healthcare, and defense deployments
  • Negative results and failure modes of interactive/LLM-driven co-design

How to Submit

The purpose of this workshop is to spark exciting discussions and share knowledge in this theme. Therefore, dual submission or re-submission of already accepted papers are permitted, provided they offer strong insight to spur discussions aligned with the workshop theme. Accepted papers will be made available through this workshop website.

Formatting Guidelines:
  • Length: At most 6 single-spaced pages (8.5" x 11").
  • Format: 10-pt font size, two-column format, including figures, tables, and references.
  • Templates: Submissions must use the LaTeX (preferred) or Word styles found here.
  • LaTeX Specifics: Use the acmart.cls template with the sigconf option and 10-pt font.
  • Review Process: This workshop follows a single-blind review process.
