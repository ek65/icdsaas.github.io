Interactive Co-Design of Safe and Assured Autonomous Systems

Workshop at CPS-Week 2026 | Saint Malo, France

About the Workshop

Cyber-physical systems (CPS), including industrial manipulators, mobile robots, autonomous vehicles, and emerging humanoid platforms, are rapidly moving from controlled laboratory settings into high-stakes application domains such as manufacturing, construction, healthcare, and defense[cite: 219]. In these environments, autonomous systems increasingly share physical workspaces with human operators and must coordinate with other autonomous agents and networked infrastructure[cite: 220]. This tight coupling between computation, physical dynamics, and human activity makes safety, robustness, and assurance central concerns rather than afterthoughts[cite: 221].

The workshop will focus on how to interactively elicit and formalize these stakeholder requirements in the design and verification of autonomous CPS[cite: 224]. Realizing the potential of LLM-driven and other interactive techniques requires carefully designed interfaces and interaction modalities spanning text, speech, diagrams, and simulations. Ultimately, the combined interface and backend must enable users to iteratively instruct, inspect, and correct formalized domain knowledge in an interpretable manner[cite: 239].
